General Questions

CardioDetect is an AI-powered web application that uses machine learning to predict heart disease risk based on medical parameters. It analyzes 11 key medical factors including age, cholesterol, blood pressure, and ECG results to provide instant risk assessments.

Our Random Forest model achieves 89.79% accuracy on our test dataset. The model was trained on 1,173 patient records with 10-fold cross-validation and tested against 12 different machine learning algorithms. Individual results may vary based on data accuracy.

Using the App

1. Go to the Predict page, 2. Fill in all 10 medical parameters, 3. Click "Get Prediction", 4. View your results with risk classification. Make sure all values are realistic and within the specified ranges for accurate results.

Input ranges are based on typical medical values. Age (1-120 years), Cholesterol (0-600 mg/dL), Heart Rate (60-220 bpm), ST Depression (0-10 mV). These ranges ensure data validity and realistic medical assessments.

Green heart icon: No heart disease detected. Red heart icon: Heart disease detected. Results include a probability score (0-100%) and risk level classification (Low, Medium, High Risk) based on the model's confidence.

Technical Details

We use a Random Forest Classifier with Gini criterion and 100 decision trees. Random Forest is an ensemble method that combines multiple decision trees for robust predictions. It was selected after testing 12 different algorithms.

The model was trained on the UCI Heart Disease dataset containing 1,173 patient records. We applied z-score outlier detection, feature normalization with MinMaxScaler, and 10-fold cross-validation. Train-test split was 80:20 with stratification.
